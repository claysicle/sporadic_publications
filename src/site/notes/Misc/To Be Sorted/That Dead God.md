I have found the silence that so many crave. 
and that I had hoped for so, so many days.
I wanted to live quietly because the storm was deafening,
and I couldn't ever make it stop because I was always questioning.

everything I knew,
everything I cared about,
that storm broke it to pieces, 
beyond every decent doubt.

it was screaming, tearing, clawing, gnashing,
wind ripping at my clothes,
hail pelting at my face,
I was frigid to my bones.

and that noise was never-ending, 
the roar would never cease,
and the storm would bring me to my knees,
Where I could only weep.

And pray a desperate prayer.
a prayer to a dead god,
I didn't think was there.

I did it anyway,
It flowed forth from my soul.
Without a single word to say,
A piece of what was whole.

A prayer of scorn
A prayer of loss
A prayer that had no meaning
A prayer I simply tossed

into void and dark
into chaos and cold 
drowned out by all the noise
the storm would keep its hold

that dead God could not hear me, 
that dead God could not care.
that dead God would not save me,
that dead God was not there.


but then I made it through.

and it was quiet.

and it was silent.


I saw on the horizon,
the place I was before,
and I knew that I had made it,
beyond that darkest storm.

The dead god rose me up, 
living and defiant.
And whether he was there or not, 
I felt a present triumph.

it was peaceful and serene,
the calm that I had hoped for.
but I can't shake the thought,
I'm here for so much more

than silence.

That dead God that had pulled me
From the wailing of the seas,
He told me there was so much more
the world could be for me.

no, I'm not here for silence,
I'm here for harmony.
Not just to escape dissonance,
But a living symphony.

A grand cacophony,
the cosmic beauty,
reverent beyond all comprehension,
passion blooming from the sea.

I'm not just here to be,
I'm not just some machine,
I won't just sit in silence,
to maintain this pleasant scene.

So the quiet's not enough,
I don't just want out of that storm.
I'll make it through the cold,
to make it to the warm.

to make it to my paradise,
it's worth it in the end.
to make it past the freezing blight,
and live how I'd intend.

That dead God brought me life,
but I won't squander it. 
I will not run from strife,
I will not simply sit.

My paradise is not silent,
it's alive and vibrant.
It's not a simple quiet, 
It's a roaring waterfall,
a rapid river running,
A view over it all.

A choir, a band, 
an orchestra, a song,
sang by rocks and trees
through the winters long.

I will not go quiet,
into that good night.
I'll be building, moving, living, breathing,
I'll fight with all my might.
I'll do it out of spite.
that storm puts up a fight,
But I'll never settle for silence,
When I could have a paradise.


So I'll enter that dark storm,

Stronger than I was before.

I'll make it through that dark storm,

I've been through it before.

And with me through that dark storm 

that dead God is my oar.

While I'm in my shambled ship,

that dead God makes me more.

that dead God keeps me warm.

Although that dark storm swells strong,

loud fast and pure,

that dead God lives within me,

and of that I am sure.


and I know that when I reach paradise,
I will live in peace forevermore.
And I know when I reach paradise,
that dead God will calm the storm.


he will walk across the waters,
he will comfort those who mourn.
Because when we think that God is dead,
he'll rise again once more.

That God you say is dead
He lives inside of me
Inside of every word
Every breath I breathe

My faith does not quiet me
It dispels no ounce of doubt
It does not make me ignorant 
Of what I am without

Its a dissonance, a contrast
A light against my dark
And through the clash of rock and steel
There will be a spark

That Ignites so bright a burning fire
Deep within my soul
Blazing passion, hope and ire
Love that overflows

The storm that I faced
Is nothing to me now
Because the storm that is within
Though I know not how

It speaks to me now
It leads me now
That dead god lives within me
And keeps with me now

That dead god, he can hear me
That dead god, he does care
That dead god, he has saved me
That dead god, he is there.

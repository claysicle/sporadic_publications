Everything is stale.
The world is dead and empty.
Even music has lost its pulse;
And I can barely feel my own.

I think I might be dead,
my spirit now has left.
I think my soul has fled,
And left me lone, bereft.

Goosebumps swell,
I feel them prickling.
I push them down.

Do I not want to feel?
Why stifle wonderment?
What is there to fear?
I fear liveliness.

Too potent,
too virile,
too imperfect,
too grating!

Passion distorts,
and now that is clear.
I am seeing things as they are,
and I am seeing nothing else.

Distilled.
Unadulterated.
Pristine.
Untouched.

My spirit is here, I find.
It is a Straight Bourbon Whiskey,
Aged in solitude;
Kept in a barrel 
In a basement –
A decade 
Distilled.

It is not stale,
It is finely refined.
It is readying its potency,
And thusly unfound.

So now I accept it –

My soul rests,
and ages for the day
when the Bourbon flows 
Once more.

When that Bourbon flows –
the goosebumps return,
My liveliness revived!

When that Bourbon flows –
although vision blurred,
I feel myself alive.

Oh, that bitter bottle!
Oh, that fire that fills.
Although my head will throttle,
I endure it, for the thrills.

My holy sacrament,
I take it with cake!
Indulgences made holy,
and all else now is fake.

The Bourbon flows,
my mind slows,
and the moment 
is suspended now.

Time is still
And the barley mill
has taken away
my frown.

So let the Bourbon flow,
Drink it down with ease.
Don’t take my Bourbon now,
Else my soul would cease.

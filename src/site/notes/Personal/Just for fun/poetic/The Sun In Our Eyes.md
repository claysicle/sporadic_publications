From *The Collected Works of Allister Hartwell*

We clawed our way from the ocean, 
And clambered our way into caves.
Upon the walls we scrawled 
The forms of our shadows.

Seeing these we understood
That The Sun must be,
And so we sought It out, 
And found Its great glory.
<font color="#ff0000">  </font>
We worshiped Its wondrous warmth,
Sang songs of Its brilliance bold,
And Its light was like a lightning 
Upon our very lips,
A language of our own.

We basked in Its glory 
From dusk until dawn,
But in the eve It fell.
…
Staggering in the darkness.
Minds untethered from our world.
Cast into dreams, reality lost.

But there were those who stayed,
The light in their eyes did not fade.
For they had seen The Sun,
And It had stayed with them.

So, when the many slept in the day,
Having gone back to their caves,
With Whom The Sun Had Stayed 
Woke them,
And brought them back into the light.

Back with The Sun,
The many danced away,
And With Whom The Sun Had Stayed… 

Sang songs of Its brilliance once more!
